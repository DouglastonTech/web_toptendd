Number Eight: African Sleeping  Disease
HOW TO CATCH IT :
Sleeping diseases occur only in 36 sub-Saharan African countries. It is spread by a fly called Tsetse fly. This is rare, as long as you don't live in one of the 36 sub-Saharan countries. In the Democratic Republic of the Congo - 80 percent of all cases come from there.

PEOPLE KILLED PER YEAR:
African Sleeping Disease kills about 300,000- 500,000 people per year.

DISCOVERED:
African Sleeping Disease was discovered in 1841.
